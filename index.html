FUNGI

I am sure you know or have heard about Fungi, tiny organisms that come in countless colors and forms. Some are found in nature and some even in our houses, but

Do you really know everything they can do?

Fungi are a group of living organisms with cells that have a nucleus (eukaryotes), and they belong to their own kingdom in nature. At first glance, they can be confused with plants because they don’t move and look similar. In fact, scientists originally classified them as plants.

However, fungi are really different, they don't have chloroplasts (the part of plant cells that allows photosynthesis and gives plants their green color). Instead of using the sun for to nourish themselves like plants, fungi gets nutrients in their unique way

Fungi are decomposters, which means they absorb nutrients from organic matter (material that comes from living organisms like plants, animals, and microorganisms). This is one of the reasons why they are so important for ecosystems.

This group is huge, with more than 144,000 known species. It includes everything from tiny fungi growing near trees, to the mushrooms we eat in pasta, to the mold that grows on old fruit. Even though we often think of fungi as small, a single fungus can actually spread for kilometers underground.
